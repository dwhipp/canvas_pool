Rules and Other Information

This pool-table simulation was created as an exercise to help me learn Javascript (and HTML Canvas animations) in November 2009. Please feel free to reuse any parts of the code that you find helpful (attribution is appreciated). Note that, because it uses the HTML Canvas element, it will not work on all browsers. I've so far only tested it on Firefox, Safari, and Chrome.

User Interface

The user-interface to this pool table is fairly straight forward:

  • First, select a game using the buttons at the top of the page: 8-Ball and 9-Ball are games with rules; 1-Ball and "Empty Table" are for practice.
  • You will start out with "ball in hand": move the cue ball using the mouse, and click to select the position. (If you get the wrong position then hit the "9" key to re-enter "ball in hand" mode)
  • Once the cue ball is positioned, click on it, and hold the button down) to begin your shot. A "cue" should appear (a line stretching back from the ball): move the mouse away from the cue ball to increase the strength. A black bar should appear in the cue to indicate the strength. You can press-and-hold a number from "1" to "7" to modulate the strength-to-distance ratio
  • Release the mouse button to make the shot.

You will most likely notice that the exact location where you click on the cue ball effects the behavior of the ball during the shot. Relative to the direction of the shot, hit-locations avoce and below the center will induce forward and reverse spin; locations left and right will add size-spin (also known as "english"). You you hold the "8" key during the shot then this will be magnified.

9-Ball

The rules of 9-ball are simple: every shot, the cue ball must impact the lowest numbered ball on the table and, after that impact, some ball must hit a cushion or be potted. If you do pot any ball (except the cue ball, which is a foul), then you remain at the table for another shot. The first player to pot the 9-ball wins.

If you commit a foul then your opponent gets ball in hand.

The ball numbers are:

  • One ball -- Yellow
  • Two ball -- Blue
  • Three ball -- Red
  • Four ball -- Purple
  • Five ball -- Orange
  • Six ball -- Green
  • Seven ball -- Brown
  • Eight ball -- Black
  • Nine ball -- Gold with white dot

8-Ball

The game of 8-ball has 7 red balls, 7 yellow balls, and a black ball (the "Eight Ball").

The game starts with an open table: the cue ball can hit and pot any ball. The first player to pot one or more red or yellow ball will own that color (if you pot balls of both color then the table remains open). The other player owns the other color. When you own a color, the cue ball must make its first impact with a ball of that color.

When you have potted all the balls of the color that you own then you may play the eight ball to win the game. If you pot the eight ball at any other time, then you lose. Also, you lose if you commit a foul on a shot where you pot the eight ball then you lose.

1-Ball, and "Empty Table"

These games are for practice: hit the ball(s) anywhere you want.
